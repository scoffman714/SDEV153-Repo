Planets and Houses in Astrology

In a birth chart, the planets represent different parts of your personality and life experiences. The houses represent the areas of life where those planetary energies are most likely to appear.

What the Planets Represent

The Sun

The Sun represents your identity, confidence, life purpose, and sense of self. Your Sun sign describes the qualities you are learning to express throughout your life.

The Moon

The Moon represents your emotions, instincts, habits, and inner needs. It can describe what makes you feel safe, comfortable, and emotionally supported.

Mercury

Mercury represents communication, thinking, learning, and how you process information. It can show how you speak, write, solve problems, and share ideas.

Venus

Venus represents love, relationships, beauty, pleasure, and personal values. It can describe what you are attracted to and how you express affection.

Mars

Mars represents action, motivation, passion, courage, and anger. It can show how you pursue goals, handle conflict, and express desire.

Jupiter

Jupiter represents growth, opportunity, optimism, wisdom, and expansion. It can show where you may experience confidence, learning, good fortune, or personal development.

Saturn

Saturn represents responsibility, discipline, boundaries, and long-term lessons. It can show where you may face challenges but also develop maturity and strength.

Uranus

Uranus represents change, freedom, individuality, originality, and unexpected events. It can show where you may challenge traditions or seek greater independence.

Neptune

Neptune represents dreams, imagination, spirituality, compassion, and illusion. It can show where you are highly sensitive, creative, or likely to idealize situations.

Pluto

Pluto represents transformation, power, healing, and rebirth. It can show where you may experience deep change and discover hidden emotional strength.

What the 12 Houses Represent

First House: Self and Identity

The First House represents your appearance, personality, first impressions, personal identity, and how you approach life.

Second House: Money and Values

The Second House represents finances, possessions, personal values, self-worth, and the resources that help you feel secure.

Third House: Communication

The Third House represents communication, learning, siblings, neighbors, short trips, and your everyday environment.

Fourth House: Home and Family

The Fourth House represents your home, family, childhood, emotional foundations, privacy, and sense of belonging.

Fifth House: Creativity and Pleasure

The Fifth House represents creativity, romance, hobbies, entertainment, self-expression, children, and the activities that bring you joy.

Sixth House: Work and Wellness

The Sixth House represents daily routines, work habits, service, health, wellness, responsibilities, and caring for the details of everyday life.

Seventh House: Partnerships

The Seventh House represents committed relationships, partnerships, marriage, agreements, and how you connect with others one-on-one.

Eighth House: Transformation

The Eighth House represents intimacy, shared finances, inheritance, emotional bonds, secrets, healing, loss, and transformation.

Ninth House: Beliefs and Exploration

The Ninth House represents higher education, travel, philosophy, religion, personal beliefs, publishing, and the search for meaning.

Tenth House: Career and Reputation

The Tenth House represents career, ambition, leadership, public reputation, achievements, and the goals you want to accomplish.

Eleventh House: Community and Goals

The Eleventh House represents friendships, groups, communities, social causes, future plans, and your hopes and dreams.

Twelfth House: Inner World

The Twelfth House represents spirituality, dreams, solitude, hidden emotions, the subconscious, healing, and private areas of life.
